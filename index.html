Food Insecurity In The US

Analyzing the current food access issue in America, its impacts, and what we can do.

What is Food Insecurity?

Lacking food availability, limited food access, and irresponsible food utilization is a scary situation.

Food access refers to having available, nutritious, and affordable food. When there is a lack of food access, the state of food insecurity and food desert rises.

Nutrition and affordability are the two components of food accessibility.

If food is affordable, it may not be nutritious enough to ensure a balanced diet.*

However, if available food is nutrient dense, the prices could be too high to purchase enough food.

*For example, fast-food and convenience stores may be available and affordable, but, they lack fresh produce, are highly processed, and often contain too much sodium or sugar.

Who is Affected?

Food insecurity affects those all around the world


Within America, this is a prevalent issue among many communities and isn't going away anytime soon. Food insecurity is highest for single-mother households and below-poverty-line households. It also affects a wide range such as urban cities, rural areas, and college students.

What are the long-lasting effects?

Impact of food choices on life expectancy

Factors such as increased sugary drink consumption, increased saturated fat consumption, and increased trans fat consumption was correlated with increased risk for diabetes, obesity, and cardiovascular disease. With these substances being cheaper and more common, food deserts have a high concentration of sugary drinks and unhealthy fats so people in these areas are at a higher risk for these adverse health conditions.
